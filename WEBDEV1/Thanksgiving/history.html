The Origins of Thanksgiving

Thanksgiving traces back to 1621, when the Pilgrims and the Wampanoag people shared a three-day feast celebrating the Pilgrims first successful harvest.

Becoming a National Holiday

Although harvest feasts continued for centuries, Thanksgiving did not become an official U.S. holiday until President Abraham Lincoln declared it in 1863 during the Civil War.

Traditions Through Time

From parades to family meals, Thanksgiving has evolved into a day centered around gratitude, celebration, and togetherness.
